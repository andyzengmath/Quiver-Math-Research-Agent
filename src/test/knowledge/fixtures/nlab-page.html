Derived category

In homological algebra and algebraic geometry, the derived category of an abelian category is a construction that provides a framework for working with chain complexes up to quasi-isomorphism. It was introduced by Jean-Louis Verdier in his 1963 thesis under the supervision of Alexander Grothendieck. The derived category D(A) of an abelian category A is obtained by formally inverting all quasi-isomorphisms in the category of chain complexes Ch(A). A quasi-isomorphism is a chain map that induces isomorphisms on all cohomology groups. The construction proceeds in two steps: first one forms the homotopy category K(A) by identifying chain-homotopic maps, then one localizes K(A) at the class of quasi-isomorphisms.

Idea

The basic idea is that many constructions in homological algebra are best understood not at the level of individual cohomology groups but at the level of chain complexes themselves. The derived category makes this precise by providing a category where quasi-isomorphic complexes become isomorphic.
